POLICY.
Last updated: April 2025
MIT Licensed · Open Source
Applies to: Drishya Android App
01
Overview

Drishya ("the App") is a free, open-source Android streaming application developed and maintained by Shashwat-CODING. This Privacy Policy explains how the App handles information when you use it.

Short version: Drishya does not collect, store, or transmit any personally identifiable information. The App is designed to function entirely without an account or sign-in.

By installing or using Drishya, you agree to this policy. If you do not agree, please uninstall the App. This policy applies solely to the official Drishya application distributed via GitHub Releases at github.com/Shashwat-CODING/Drishya.

02
Data We Collect

Drishya collects no personal data from users. We do not operate any servers, databases, or analytics pipelines that receive user information. Specifically:

  • No account required. You do not need to register, log in, or provide any personal details to use the App.
  • No analytics or telemetry. The App does not embed crash reporting SDKs, usage analytics, or advertising frameworks.
  • No device fingerprinting. We do not collect device identifiers, hardware IDs, or fingerprints of any kind.
  • No location data. The App never requests or uses GPS, network-based, or coarse location permissions.
  • No contact or media access. Drishya does not access your contacts, gallery, camera, or microphone.
03
What We Don't Collect

To be explicit, Drishya never collects or processes:

  • Name, email address, phone number, or any contact information
  • IP address or geolocation data logged on our end
  • Browsing history, search queries, or watch history transmitted externally
  • Payment information of any kind (the App is free)
  • Biometric data or sensitive personal attributes
  • Social media credentials or OAuth tokens

All watch history, preferences, and configuration you set in the App are stored exclusively on your own device using Android Shared Preferences and are never sent anywhere.

04
Third-Party Services

Drishya connects to external streaming providers and backend instances that you configure. When you stream content, your device makes network requests to those services. Those services operate under their own privacy policies, which are separate from this document.

  • Configured providers. Any backend URL you set via the Configure page is stored locally and used solely to fetch streaming content on your behalf.
  • Content delivery networks. Video streams and metadata may be served by CDNs operated by third parties. Standard HTTP request headers (including your IP address) are sent as part of normal network operation — this is outside our control.
  • GitHub Releases API. The App's download page queries the GitHub API to display the latest release version. No user data is sent in this request beyond standard HTTP headers.
  • Image caching. Poster images and thumbnails are fetched from external URLs (e.g. TMDB CDN) and cached locally on your device using cached_network_image.

We are not responsible for the privacy practices of third-party providers. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any external services you connect Drishya to.

05
Local Storage

Some data is stored locally on your device to improve your experience. This data never leaves your device:

  • Watch history. Titles you have watched and resume positions are saved via Android Shared Preferences.
  • Provider configuration. The backend URL(s) you configure are stored locally so the App can reconnect on launch.
  • Image cache. Thumbnails and posters are cached on-disk to reduce bandwidth on repeat visits.
  • App preferences. UI settings such as subtitle style, preferred audio track, or theme preferences.

You can clear all locally stored data at any time by clearing the App's storage via Android Settings → Apps → Drishya → Storage → Clear Data, or by uninstalling the App.

06
Network Requests

Drishya makes outbound network requests only for the following purposes:

  • Fetching stream URLs and metadata from your configured provider backend
  • Loading poster/thumbnail images from content metadata endpoints
  • Buffering and playing video/audio streams via media_kit
  • Checking for new App releases via the GitHub Releases API (on the website, not in-app)

All requests are initiated by you through your own use of the App. The App does not "phone home" to any Drishya-operated server at startup, in the background, or at any other time.

07
Children's Privacy

Drishya is a general-purpose streaming application and is not directed at children under the age of 13. Because the App does not collect any personal information from any user regardless of age, it does not knowingly collect data from children.

Content available through configured streaming providers may not be appropriate for all ages. Parents and guardians are responsible for supervising their children's use of the App and the content accessed through it.

08
Policy Changes

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected by an updated "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of Drishya after a policy update constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.

Since Drishya is open-source, you can always review the full history of this policy and all App code at the GitHub repository.
